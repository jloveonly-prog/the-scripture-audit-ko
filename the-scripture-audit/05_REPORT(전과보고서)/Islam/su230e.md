### 🧠 Logic/Philosophy Terminology 

Must-know logical/academic terms needed to follow the arguments in this document.

- **Premise:** The foundational thought or condition laid out before arriving at a conclusion. (Ex: The premise 'when it rains, the ground gets wet' must exist to reach the conclusion 'the ground is wet, so it must have rained.')
- **Inference/Deduction:** Reaching a new factual understanding concerning what is unknown logically based on known information.
- **Redundancy/Tautology:** Meaninglessly repeating words or phrases with identical meaning twice. (Ex: 'Free gift', 'AT machine'). Used in the argument that if 'Corruption (Fasad)' and 'Blood (Dam)' hold identical meaning, the Quran has committed the fallacy of tautology.
- **Epistemological:** Pertaining to 'how' humans (or angels) 'perceive and acquire knowledge of' something. 'Epistemologically impossible' means 'due to the intrinsic limitations of that being, there exists no method whatsoever to fathom it intellectually.'
- **Derivation:** A new concept branching off and originating from a foundation (original). A 'derived concept' cannot spawn without a 'primitive concept'.
- **Rhetorical/Figure of speech:** Literary 'decorating' techniques employed not merely to deliver cold facts, but to amplify the intensity of a phrase or word to deeply stir or evoke emotion.
- **Self-contradiction:** A situation where one's own words or actions contradict one another, resulting in an attack or disparagement of oneself.
